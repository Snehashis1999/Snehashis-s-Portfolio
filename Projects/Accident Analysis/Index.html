Project Overview

Road accidents remain one of India's most significant public safety challenges, resulting in substantial loss of life, economic productivity and infrastructure costs every year.

This project analyses accident data collected between 2021 and 2024 to identify long-term trends, regional accident hotspots and behavioural, demographic and environmental factors contributing to accidents.

Following a complete Business Intelligence workflow, the project transforms raw government datasets into interactive dashboards and policy-oriented recommendations for improving road safety.

Business Objectives

📍 Accident Hotspots

Identify states with the highest accident frequency using both absolute and population-adjusted metrics.

🚗 Driver Behaviour

Analyse overspeeding, licensing status, age groups and behavioural factors.

🛣 Infrastructure

Study accident distribution across highways, urban roads, junctions and road types.

🌦 Environment

Evaluate weather, collision type, lighting conditions and time of accidents.

📊 Policy Support

Generate data-driven recommendations for improving road safety.

Key Insights

The analysis identified critical trends across accident frequency, infrastructure, driver behaviour and regional risk patterns.

📈 National Trend

  • Road accidents increased by 18.4% between 2021 and 2024.
  • Although accidents continue to rise, annual growth has gradually slowed.
  • Fatal accidents continue to remain a major public safety concern.

🚗 Driver Behaviour

  • Overspeeding accounts for nearly 75% of violation-related accidents.
  • Male drivers dominate accident involvement.
  • Drivers aged 25–35 years represent the highest risk group.

🛣 Infrastructure

  • National Highways record the highest fatality rate.
  • Road design and junction characteristics strongly influence accident severity.
  • Infrastructure quality remains a major contributor to road safety.

📍 Geographic Analysis

  • Tamil Nadu reports the highest total accidents.
  • Goa records the highest accident density per lakh population.
  • Regional disparities indicate the need for state-specific interventions.
